My First Memories

I was born on a cold winter morning on the 26 of December in the year One Thousand eight hundred and Seven of our Lord. My parents named mee after my Father Robert who was a Butcher and Farmer in Gloucester. My Mother was Sarah Oakey.

As a boy i helped my Father in his Shoppe. He taught mee how to know which cows had the best meats and how to separate clean and prepare the cuts. We had a happy life though wee worked hard living among the fields and hamlets in the area known as Gloucestershire.

Jul 1825

I often go to play with the other boys in the streets. We've become fond of Pugilism - fighting honourably in a ring with one's fists. Except wee haven't got a ring. Just us boys geting dirty and scratched and scolded by our mums. I am the strongest of all! There is none that can fell mee!

My sister Elizabeth who is younger than i often comes with mee to fight. She encourages mee yelling from the crowds.

"At him again, at him again, Bobbie. Pop it to him. Aye, that’s the way. it’s some of the ‘Arris blood croppin’ out."

Aug, 1830

I've quickly become the greatest Pugilist in the shire! I no longer fight in the streets, but at the fairs, and for prize-money! Someday i hope to fight in the honorable rings of London town.

Jan 1834

Elizabeth has fallen into the afections of one of the Browett boys and was married to him recently. Daniel is a good man - a gentleman and a fellow Brother in our Methodist congregation. I've known him for some time.

I myself have recently grown very fond of a young Woman by the name of Hannah Mariah Eagles. If her feelings are a reflection of my own i may ask for her hand!

September 1835

My Deare Hannah has accepted my hand in marriage!

As both Hannah and i feel partial toward the Methodist movement wee petitiond a minister of that congregation to perform our rites early this spring. We passed a happy summer together until word began to spread of our marriage. Many of the older generation turnd hostile toward us for English law only considers marriage to be legal if performed by the Church of England.

To apease the law and our neighbours wee posted banns in the church and were married again by the Church of England on 28 of September in the Parish of St Mary de Lode. Elizabeth and Daniel acted as witnesses to the event.

All this commotion has raised several questions in my mind. Why should there be Churches thus which war with each other over authority? Is there any one Church that can claim the proper authority to perform a marriage?

Letter to Hannah

My Beloved Wife i made out to send you twenty Four Dollers by the agent that was apointed to recieve the moneys for the church and i gave to Brother Leo. and Leegan something towards asisting them Back to the camp of Jorab wheare where you are my only Deare i commenced this Letter on the 19 of September and being disapointed of my Money which it caused mee to greave but the Lord as it ternd turned out so well as it as i could not get no more for Love nor money for i have yused all my Influence to get so much as i did but i hope you will come with the first company in the spring that wee may meet one a nother again in that Land which is apointed for the faithful please to remember mee to Brother Bundry and Family and to Brothers Horn, Br Orr and Russell and all the Twelve and Every Othere good faithful man and Woman, my Love please to excuse mee i was ablighte obliged to scribble pretty fast to get this done at the last … for wee expect to march instantly my Love I am injoying good health and I hope you and my Deare children do the same please to Rite and Send mee word as soon as you can how you are and how you are getting along and … Brother Elias and Orgery and Bloxham so no more at Presant From your true Lover and afectuate Husband Robert Harris and my prayer is that the great God of Heaven will persearve preserve you in the Hallow of is hand for Ever Amen and Amen I do not know how to tell you to direct the Letters to mee no more than to R Harris 5 Company Mormon Battalion - Upper California
